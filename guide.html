‚úÖ Tasks

Log every significant work activity with three key metrics.

How to Log a Task

  1. Enter task name (be specific: "Q3 budget analysis" not just "finance work")
  2. Rate Intensity (1-10): How mentally/emotionally difficult?
  3. Rate ROI (1-10): How valuable to your goals?
  4. Rate Burn (1-10): How much energy did it take?
  5. Check "Frog" if this is your hardest task of the day
  6. Choose action: Keep doing, Delegate, Automate, or Eliminate

üìè Detailed Scoring Guide

Consistent scoring is crucial for accurate insights.

Intensity Scale (Difficulty)

  • 1: Mindless - Could do while sleeping (copying, filing)
  • 2: Trivial - Basic routine (email, scheduling)
  • 3: Easy - Some attention needed (data entry, simple calls)
  • 4: Light - Mild focus (routine reports, standard meetings)
  • 5: Average - Normal work (regular projects, planning)
  • 6: Moderate - Requires concentration (analysis, writing)
  • 7: Challenging - Stretches abilities (complex problems, negotiations)
  • 8: Hard - Near limit (major presentations, difficult decisions)
  • 9: Extreme - Maximum capability (crisis management, breakthroughs)
  • 10: Impossible - Beyond current ability (bet-the-company decisions)

ROI Scale (Value)

  • 1: Waste - Should not be done
  • 2: Minimal - Barely worth doing
  • 3: Low - Nice to have
  • 4: Below Average - Some value
  • 5: Average - Standard return
  • 6: Above Average - Good value
  • 7: High - Significant impact
  • 8: Very High - Major wins
  • 9: Critical - Game-changing
  • 10: Maximum - Career/Life defining

Burn Scale (Energy Cost)

  • 1: Energizing - Gives you energy
  • 2: Refreshing - Easy and enjoyable
  • 3: Light - Minimal effort
  • 4: Mild - Some energy needed
  • 5: Average - Normal tiredness
  • 6: Noticeable - Feel the drain
  • 7: Taxing - Need a break after
  • 8: Exhausting - Need significant recovery
  • 9: Depleting - Wipes you out
  • 10: Total Burnout - Cannot continue

üèÜ Best Practices

Daily Routine

  1. Morning (First 30 min): Review yesterday's metrics, identify today's frog
  2. Before Noon: Complete your frog task
  3. Throughout Day: Log tasks immediately after completion
  4. Evening: Review day's performance, plan tomorrow's frog

Weekly Routine

  1. Monday: Review last week's insights, set week goals
  2. Wednesday: Mid-week check on intensity average
  3. Friday: Identify energy vampires to eliminate
  4. Sunday: Plan next week's frogs

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Rating everything as 5 (be honest and specific)
  • Forgetting to mark frog tasks
  • Logging tasks days later (memory fades)
  • Ignoring burn scores (leads to burnout)
  • Not celebrating PRs (motivation matters)

Success Indicators

  • Average intensity consistently at/above 8
  • Frog streak longer than 7 days
  • More "Keep" than "Eliminate" decisions
  • PR rate of 2-3 per week
  • Energy vampires decreasing over time

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

What exactly is a "Frog" task?

Your most important AND most difficult task of the day. The one you're most likely to procrastinate on but will have the biggest positive impact.

How many tasks should I log daily?

Log 3-7 significant tasks. Don't log every tiny action, but don't miss important work either.

What if I can't hit intensity 8?

Either you need harder challenges (ask for stretch projects) or you're underrating your current work. Most knowledge work should hit 6-8 regularly.

How do I know if I have a PR?

The app auto-detects PRs when you hit a new maximum intensity with ROI ‚â• 8. You'll see a celebration!

Should I log failed attempts?

Yes! Rate based on effort, not outcome. Failed attempts at intensity 9 tasks still count.

What's the ideal Burn level?

Aim for average burn of 5-6. Too low means you're coasting; too high means burnout risk.
